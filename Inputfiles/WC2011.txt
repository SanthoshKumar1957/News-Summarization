The ICC Cricket World Cup 2011 was the 10th edition of the Cricket World Cup, and it was co-hosted by India, Sri Lanka, and Bangladesh. The final of the ICC Cricket World Cup 2011 was held on April 2, 2011, at the Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai, India. The final match was played between India and Sri Lanka. India won the final match by defeating Sri Lanka. India successfully chased down Sri Lanka's target of 275 runs with 10 balls to spare. Gautam Gambhir and MS Dhoni played crucial innings for India. Gautam Gambhir scored 97 runs, and MS Dhoni scored an unbeaten 91 runs, leading India to victory. This victory was significant for India as it marked their second Cricket World Cup win, with their first win being in 1983. The 2011 World Cup victory was celebrated widely across India, as cricket holds a special place in the country's sports culture.
